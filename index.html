Why read this book

Courage is a professional skill. This book teaches it.

◆

A blueprint for trust in the age of AI

As artificial intelligence reshapes how decisions get made, assurance has never mattered more. Learn what auditors and leaders must do to keep technology — and the institutions that rely on it — honest.

◆

Real stories, hard-won lessons

Drawing on the author's own experience under fire — including being publicly challenged by U.S. senators — this is a candid look at the true cost of silence and the power of speaking up.

◆

A practical playbook, not just theory

A clear decision framework and concrete tools for navigating ethical gray zones, abusive authority, and high-stakes moments where staying quiet is the easy path.

◆

Built for the next generation

Whether you're a student, a CPA, a board member, or a leader, discover how to build cultures where courage is the norm — and train the professionals our future depends on.

“Silence has consequences. So does courage — and this book is about choosing the second.”

About the author

C. Christina Ho, CPA, CISA

Christina Ho grew up in Macau, the daughter of a family that believed in hard work, education, and doing what is right even when it costs you something. She came to the United States for college at eighteen and built a career spanning public accounting, federal government, and financial regulation — becoming the first Asian American appointed to the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board, where she cast four historic dissenting votes and spoke truth to power at the highest levels of government.

She is now working with an AI technology startup to build the audit firm of the future and is passionate about leveraging data and technology for the public good. Her professional writing has appeared in the Journal of Accountancy and other leading publications; she has been profiled in The Wall Street Journal and quoted by Bloomberg, Reuters, and The Financial Times.

Outside the office, Christina enjoys learning and writing about technology, wellness, parenting, and faith. She lives in Washington, D.C. with her daughter, who inspires everything.
